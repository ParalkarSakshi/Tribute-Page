Padma Bhushan. Ratan Tata

The man who revolutionized Indian industry and philanthropy

Here's a time line of Ratan's' life:

  • 1937 - Born in Bombay (now Mumbai), India, into the prominent Tata family
  • 1955 – Graduates from the prestigious Campion School in Mumbai
  • 1959 - Earns a degree in architecture from Cornell University, USA
  • 1961 - Joins Tata Steel, working on the shop floor alongside blue-collar employees to learn the business from the ground up
  • 1975 - Completes the Advanced Management Program from Harvard Business School 1991 – Becomes Chairman of Tata Sons, succeeding JRD Tata; under his leadership, Tata Group expands its global footprint
  • 1998 - Launches Tata Indica, India’s first indigenously developed passenger car, marking a new era for Indian automobile manufacturing
  • 2000 - Acquires UK-based Tetley Tea, marking one of India’s first major overseas acquisitions
  • 2004 - Tata Motors acquires South Korean truck manufacturer Daewoo, strengthening Tata’s global presence in the automotive sector
  • 2007 - Makes history by purchasing British luxury brands Jaguar and Land Rover (JLR) from Ford, solidifying Tata Motors' global standing in the automotive industry
  • 2008 - Introduces the Tata Nano, the world’s cheapest car, aimed at making car ownership affordable for millions of Indians
  • 2012 - Steps down as Chairman of Tata Group after turning 75, handing over leadership to Cyrus Mistry (who was later replaced by N. Chandrasekaran in 2017)
  • 2020 - During the COVID-19 pandemic, pledges ₹1,500 crore (approx. $200 million) through Tata Trusts and Tata Group companies to support India’s fight against the virus
  • 2021 - Honored with the Order of Australia for his service to the India-Australia relationship
  • 2022 - Becomes one of the most beloved public figures in India, known for his humility, philanthropy, and ethical business practices
